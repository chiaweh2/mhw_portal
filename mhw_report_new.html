Global Marine Heatwave Forecast Discussion

Observed and forecasted values include the effects of long-term warming. Values with the long-term warming trend removed are in brackets.

Current marine heatwave conditions:

In May 2024, 37% [24%] of the global ocean experienced MHWs, which ranked 7th [8th] among all months since 1991.

While the fraction of the globe with marine heatwave (MHW) conditions remained relatively unchanged from April, those along the equator in the tropical Pacific decreased as El Niño weakened. MHWs are currently found in the tropical Pacific except near the equator east of the dateline, across much of Pacific to the east of Japan, the Sea of Japan and South China Sea, much of the tropical and eastern Atlantic, the Gulf of Mexico, the Caribbean Sea, south of Iceland and around the British Isles, much of the central and western tropical Indian Ocean, and portions of the Atlantic, Indian and Pacific sectors of the Southern Ocean.

Marine heatwave forecasts:

Forecasts predict that MHW coverage and overall intensity will continue to decline in the coming months (especially in the eastern and central tropical Pacific and Atlantic basins), dropping to ~29% [15%] of the global oceans experiencing MHW conditions by October 2024. Below is a regionally refined focus:

  • Tropical Pacific - MHW conditions, associated with a dissipating El Niño event, are predicted to continue to decrease over most of the equatorial Pacific, with little chance of a MHW in the eastern ~⅔ of the basin (0-10% [0-10%]; high confidence). However, MHWs are forecasted to decrease but still persist in a band extending southeastward from southeast Asia to the central south Pacific by October (50-70% [10-30%]; low-medium confidence).
  • North Pacific - Forecasts show limited risk of MHW conditions along the west coast of North America through fall (0-20% [0-10%]; low-medium confidence). In the central North Pacific, MHWs are forecasted to persist through November (40-70% [20-40%]; low confidence), as are MHWs near Japan (40-80% [20-60%]; low-medium confidence).
  • Tropical Atlantic - MHW conditions in the tropical North Atlantic are likely to persist through October 2024 (80-100% [50-80%]; low-medium confidence). MHWs in the tropical South Atlantic are expected to diminish through austral (southern) winter.
  • Caribbean Sea - MHW conditions are forecasted to persist in the Caribbean Sea through October (70-100% [50-80%]; low-medium confidence).
  • North Atlantic - While predicted to decrease, there is still a relatively high probability that MHW conditions will persist through summer in the northeast Atlantic from Northern Africa to Europe and extending northwestward to southeast Greenland (30-60% [20-60%]; low confidence). There is an elevated risk of MHWs along portions of the east coast and the Gulf Stream/North Atlantic Current through September 2024, (30-80% [20-50%]; low confidence).
  • Indian Ocean - Elevated chances of MHW conditions are predicted over much of the Indian Ocean north of ~25°S through September 2024 (40-100% [10-40%]; low-medium confidence), with the highest probabilities off the east African coast north of Madagascar.
  • Southern Ocean - MHWs in the Southern Ocean are forecasted to occur south of Africa, south of Australia and in the central Pacific (70-100% [50-90%]; low confidence).

What has been changed to calculate the global area percentage of MHW?

The methodology employed prior to September 2023 for global marine heatwaves area percentage was a probabilistic approach. This approach involved identifying ocean areas with a forecasted probability exceeding 30%. Subsequently, this identified region was utilized to compute the percentage of the ocean area encompassed by marine heatwaves.

In contrast, the new methodology utilizes a deterministic approach to calculate the area percentage. It involves the identification of marine heatwaves in each ensemble member and model over the ocean (i.e., a binary 0 or 1). The area covered by marine heatwaves is then employed to determine the percentage of ocean area affected by marine heatwaves in each ensemble member and model. To provide a consolidated view, a multi-model mean of the area percentage is calculated, which offers insight into the overall percentage of ocean area affected by marine heatwaves.

How confidence levels are determined?

Forecast statement confidence levels are based on historical performance of forecasts in each region, as measured by the Symmetrical Extremal Dependence Index (SEDI). Confidence levels are “high” (SEDI = 0.66-1), “medium” (SEDI 0.33-0.66), and “low” (SEDI 0-0.33). See historical MHW skill maps for forecast confidence as a function of lead month.
