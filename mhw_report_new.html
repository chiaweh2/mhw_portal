Global Marine Heatwave Forecast Discussion

Observed and forecasted values include the effects of long-term warming. Values with the long-term warming trend removed are in brackets.

Current marine heatwave conditions:

In August 2024, 31% [19%] of the global ocean experienced MHWs, which ranked 14th [30th] among all months since 1991.

The fraction of the globe with marine heatwave (MHW) conditions continued to decrease in August, as El Niño continues to weaken and neutral/La Niña conditions develop. MHWs are currently found in the western tropical Pacific, across much of the North Pacific to the east of Japan and in the Sea of Japan, the North Atlantic, the Gulf of Mexico, the Caribbean Sea, parts of the Norwegian and Mediterranean Seas, the Indian Ocean over the equatorial region, and some portions of the Atlantic, Indian and Pacific sectors of the Southern Ocean.

Marine heatwave forecasts:

Forecasts predict that MHW coverage and overall intensity will continue to decline in the coming months, dropping to ~27% [13%] of the global oceans experiencing MHW conditions by November 2024. Below is a regionally refined focus:

  • Pacific - Probabilities of MHWs in the eastern/central tropical Pacific and eastern extratropical Pacific are predicted to continue lower through boreal (northern hemisphere) fall/winter with only a 0-10% [0-10%] chance of MHWs in those regions (high confidence). In the Western tropical Pacific and central/western extratropical Pacific, elevated MHW probabilities are more widespread, reaching 60-100% ([10-60%]; low-medium confidence) by the end of the year.
  • Tropical Atlantic - MHW conditions in the tropical North Atlantic are likely to persist through the end of 2024 (60-90% [30-60%]; low-medium confidence), with lower MHW likelihoods in the Gulf of Mexico (40-60% [10-30%]; low confidence).
  • North Atlantic - Likelihood of MHWs in the central North Atlantic is predicted to fall over the next several months, though remaining elevated (30-80% [10-60%]; low confidence) through December.
  • Indian Ocean - Elevated chances of MHW conditions are predicted through October (50-70% [10-40%]; low-medium confidence) but slowly declined in the following months over central Indian Ocean and Indo-Pacific region, along the southeast African coast, and in the northern Arabian Sea and Bay of Bengal.
  • Southern Ocean - Elevated MHW likelihoods in the Southern Ocean are forecasted to continue through at least December in regions south of Africa and south of Australia (60-100% [40-90%]; low-medium confidence).

What has been changed to calculate the global area percentage of MHW?

The methodology employed prior to September 2023 for global marine heatwaves area percentage was a probabilistic approach. This approach involved identifying ocean areas with a forecasted probability exceeding 30%. Subsequently, this identified region was utilized to compute the percentage of the ocean area encompassed by marine heatwaves.

In contrast, the new methodology utilizes a deterministic approach to calculate the area percentage. It involves the identification of marine heatwaves in each ensemble member and model over the ocean (i.e., a binary 0 or 1). The area covered by marine heatwaves is then employed to determine the percentage of ocean area affected by marine heatwaves in each ensemble member and model. To provide a consolidated view, a multi-model mean of the area percentage is calculated, which offers insight into the overall percentage of ocean area affected by marine heatwaves.

How confidence levels are determined?

Forecast statement confidence levels are based on historical performance of forecasts in each region, as measured by the Symmetrical Extremal Dependence Index (SEDI). Confidence levels are “high” (SEDI = 0.66-1), “medium” (SEDI 0.33-0.66), and “low” (SEDI 0-0.33). See historical MHW skill maps for forecast confidence as a function of lead month.
