Global Marine Heatwave Forecast Discussion - August 2024

Observed and forecasted values include the effects of long-term warming since 1990. Values with the long-term warming trend removed are in brackets.

Current marine heatwave conditions:        

In July 2024, 35% [21%] of the global ocean experienced MHWs, which ranked 14th [32nd] among all months since 1991.

The fraction of the globe with marine heatwave (MHW) conditions continued to decrease in July, as El Niño continues to weaken and neutral/La Niña conditions develop. MHWs are currently found in the western tropical Pacific, across much of the North Pacific to the east of Japan and in the Sea of Japan, the tropical Atlantic, the Gulf of Mexico, the Caribbean Sea, parts of the Norwegian and Mediterranean Seas, much of the Indian Ocean, and portions of the Atlantic, Indian and Pacific sectors of the Southern Ocean.

Marine heatwave forecasts:

Forecasts predict that MHW coverage and overall intensity will continue to decline in the coming months, dropping to ~30% [15%] of the global oceans experiencing MHW conditions by October 2024. Below is a regionally refined focus:

  • Pacific - Probabilities of MHWs in the eastern/central tropical Pacific and eastern extratropical Pacific are predicted to decline through boreal (northern hemisphere) fall/winter with only a 0-10% [0-10%] chance of MHWs in those regions (high confidence). In the Western tropical Pacific and central/western extratropical Pacific, elevated MHW probabilities are more widespread, reaching 60-100% ([10-60%]; low-medium confidence) by the end of the year.
  • Tropical Atlantic - MHW conditions in the tropical North Atlantic are likely to persist through the end of 2024 (60-90% [30-60%]; low-medium confidence), with lower MHW likelihoods in the Gulf of Mexico (40-60% [10-30%]; low confidence).
  • North Atlantic - Likelihood of MHWs in the central North Atlantic is predicted to fall over the next several months, though remaining elevated (30-60% [30-60%]; low confidence) through November.
  • Indian Ocean - Elevated chances of MHW conditions are predicted through November in the central Indian Ocean and Indo-Pacific region, along the southeast African coast, and in the northern Arabian Sea and Bay of Bengal (50-70% [10-40%]; low-medium confidence).
  • Southern Ocean - Elevated MHW likelihoods in the Southern Ocean are forecasted to continue through at least October in regions south of Africa, south of Australia and in the central Pacific (60-100% [40-90%]; low-medium confidence).
